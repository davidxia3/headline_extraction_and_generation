Headline 1: Council approves $5.4 million increase for Liberty Ave Highway Safety Improvement Program
Summary 1: - City Council approved a $5.4 million increase in funding for the Liberty Ave Highway Safety Improvement Program (HSIP), raising the total authorized amount to $12,905,556.  
- The additional funds will support the Right of Way and Construction phases of the project, with specific allocations from Federal Highway Administration (FHWA) funds and City bonds across 2024 and 2025 budget years.  
- The municipal share of Commonwealth Incurred Costs also increased by $24,200, bringing the City’s maximum cost to $29,700.  
- The Mayor and the Director of the Department of Mobility and Infrastructure are authorized to enter into amended reimbursement agreements with the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation to cover these costs.  
- Future resolutions will address project-specific agreements related to this funding increase.

Headline 2: Council approves post-agenda meetings on Oakland BID and Affordable Housing Bond
Summary 2: - Councilwoman Strasburger motioned to hold a post-agenda meeting on the Oakland Business Improvement District (BID), which was approved unanimously.  
- Council President Lavelle motioned for a post-agenda meeting on the Affordable Housing Bond, also approved unanimously.  
- The post-agenda meetings will provide opportunities for detailed discussions on the Oakland BID and how Affordable Housing Bond funds are being used and deployed.  
- Council members emphasized the importance of reviewing the annual report on the Affordable Housing Bond to ensure transparency and effective use of funds.  
- Scheduling of both post-agenda meetings will be coordinated with the respective council members.

Headline 3: Council repeals 2019 resolution allowing Urban Redevelopment Authority to acquire city property in 15th Ward
Summary 3: - The City Council repealed Resolution 366 of 2019, which had authorized the Urban Redevelopment Authority (URA) of Pittsburgh to acquire the City’s rights to a publicly owned property in the 15th Ward (Block 56-B, Lot 345, Council District 5) at no cost to the City.  
- The repeal means the URA will no longer acquire or hold any interest in this property.  
- This action was formalized through Resolution 2025-1683 and referred to the Committee on Intergovernmental and Educational Affairs.  
- The repeal is effective retroactively from June 14, 2019, the original effective date of the prior resolution.
